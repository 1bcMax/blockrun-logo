## The Format: Three-Part Structure

### Part 1: The Update (2-3 minutes max)

**Your opening:**

"Hey Ren, thanks for making time. Quick update on where we are:

We went live on Base in January - the AI Gateway is processing real transactions with 31 models. We've got an autonomous Polymarket agent that's been trading 24/7 with real USDC - it manages its own wallet, uses our gateway for analysis, and places trades completely autonomously.

On the Coinbase side, we got our PR merged into the x402 repo and have feature requests open, so we're actively contributing to the protocol. We're also monitoring 618+ services daily and published the 'State of x402' report that got about 2,000 engagements.

We're opening up the $2M seed round now, and I wanted to see if Electric wants to participate."

**Then stop talking.** Let him respond.

---

### Part 2: The Conversation (20-40 minutes)

**He'll ask questions. Answer them. Go deep.**

#### Two modes you'll toggle between:

**Mode 1: Technical Deep Dive** (He'll probe your methodology)
- How do you detect fraud?
- What's your uptime scoring approach?
- Why can't Coinbase build this?
- What's your competitive moat?

**How to respond:**
- Be specific and technical
- Use your Circle/Capital One experience as proof points
- Show the live products (screen share if needed)
- Reference the 700+ services analysis
- **Don't bullshit** - if you don't know, say "good question, here's how we're thinking about it"

**Mode 2: Market/Business** (He'll test your thinking)
- How does this scale?
- What's the revenue model long-term?
- Who are your real competitors?
- What's the Phase 3 vision?

**How to respond:**
- Connect back to infrastructure thesis ("not an app, it's a layer")
- Show the unit economics (simple math)
- Reference Electric's portfolio companies if relevant ("similar to how [X] positioned...")
- Be honest about challenges

---

### Part 3: The Ask (Last 5 minutes)

**When the conversation winds down, make it clear:**

"So to be direct: we're raising $2M for a 24-month runway to become the default trust layer for x402. Would Electric be interested in leading or co-leading this round?"

**Or if you want to gauge interest first:**

"What would Electric need to see to participate in this round?"

**Then:**
- If interested ‚Üí Schedule next steps
- If not ‚Üí Ask what would change their mind
- If maybe ‚Üí Ask what additional info they need

## Handling Specific Scenarios

### Scenario 1: "Why can't Coinbase just build this?"

**Good answer:**
"They're focused on the protocol layer - payment rails. They need a neutral third party for trust ratings. Same reason FICO is independent of banks. Plus, we're already collaborating with them - we have merged PRs and they're engaging with our feature requests. They see us as complementary, not competitive."

**Then show**: Your GitHub PRs in their repo

---

### Scenario 2: "What if someone forks your code and competes on price?"

**Good answer:**
"The code isn't the moat - the methodology is. We're building 18 months of historical uptime data by mid-2027. A competitor starting in Q3 2026 can see the same blockchain data, but they can't replicate 8 months of latency distributions and calibrated fraud signals. Trust infrastructure is built through track record, not code clones. It's exactly like your Codeslaw tool - the code is visible, the insight is in the analysis."

**Why this works**: References his own work (Codeslaw), shows you understand technical moats

---

### Scenario 3: "How do you know developers will integrate this?"

**Good answer:**
"We're already in conversations with Coinbase AgentKit, GOAT SDK has 150K downloads, and nofx has 10K GitHub stars. We're not selling to end users - we're shipping code to repositories. Developers discover us through their tools. Each integration is distribution to their entire developer base. That's how infrastructure scales."

**Then show**: Your partnership pipeline or integration roadmap

---

### Scenario 4: "What's your revenue model long-term?"

**Good answer:**
"Two streams: AI Gateway is 5% markup - proves we deliver and generates cash now. Trust Routing is 5% fee on routed volume - that's where we scale. At 100M monthly transactions, that's $500K-$1M MRR. Simple unit economics: $0.10 average transaction, $0.005 fee per transaction. We're capturing margin on the growth curve."

**Then show**: Page 12 of your deck with the revenue table

## Post-Meeting Actions

### If Interested:
**Within 2 hours:**
- Send thank you email with links to live products
- Offer to send detailed materials
- Propose next steps (team meeting, diligence timeline)

**Within 24 hours:**
- Send requested materials (metrics, technical docs, etc.)
- Make relevant intros (co-founder, technical advisors)

### If Not Interested:
**Within 24 hours:**
- Thank you email
- Ask for specific feedback: "What would need to change for Electric to get involved?"
- Request intros: "Who else should I be talking to?"
- Stay in touch with monthly updates

### If Maybe/Need More Info:
**Within 2 hours:**
- Send exactly what they asked for (no more, no less)
- Propose timeline for follow-up
- Ask about decision timeline
