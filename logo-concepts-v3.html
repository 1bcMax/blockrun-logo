Rhodonea

The four-petal rose curve (rhodonea) — one of the most beautiful polar curves in mathematics. Created by the equation r = cos(2θ), it produces four perfectly symmetric petals that touch at the origin. Named from the Greek "rhodon" (rose). Pure mathematical elegance.

The Rhodonea curve follows r = a·cos(kθ). With k=2, this creates exactly 4 petals at 45° intervals. First studied by Luigi Guido Grandi in 1723. The shape appears in physics (electron orbitals) and antenna radiation patterns. Mathematical beauty that transcends time.
