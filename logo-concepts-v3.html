Borromean Rings

Three circles interlocked such that no two are linked — remove one, and the other two fall apart. A symbol of strength through unity, used since ancient times. The topological equivalent of "stronger together." Perfect for representing interconnected systems.

Hopf Link

Two circles linked together — the simplest non-trivial link in topology. Each ring passes through the other exactly once. Discovered by Heinz Hopf. The mathematical representation of two entities bound together. Connection made visual.
